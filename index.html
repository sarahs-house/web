About Sarah

Social science researcher-in-training, passionate about families, fertility, the life course, and the future of the American economy.

Hi, I'm Sarah! I'm a PhD Student in Sociology at the University of Michigan and predoctoral trainee at the world-renowned Population Studies Center at UofM's Institute for Social Research.

Since beginning my PhD, my work has focused on understanding contemporary processes of family formation and dissolution in the United States, with a focus on how economic uncertainty shapes individuals' decisions about whether and when to have children. I have presented my findings at the Population Association of America and the American Sociological Association annual meetings in 2025 and 2026.

I earned my undergraduate degree in 2023 from Johns Hopkins University, where I studied economics and international studies. Afterward, I worked as a predoctoral research fellow for the Poverty and Inequality Research Lab (PIRL). During my two years at PIRL, I traveled the United States, sitting down with dozens of Americans to hear their life stories and understand the mechanisms driving or inhibiting economic mobility in our country.

Beyond family demography, my research interests include survey methodology, non-parametric modeling, and Marxist feminist theory. Read about my projects for more information. Check back soon for more!
